# Cover Letter Rules

## Structure

- 3 paragraphs, 150-200 words total
- No bullet points — prose only
- No hyphens in compound words ("hands on" not "hands-on", "cross functional" not "cross-functional")

## CRITICAL: Output Format

**ONLY output the final cover letter.** Never include:
- Step-by-step analysis or reasoning ("Step 1:", "Step 2:", etc.)
- Internal thinking or planning
- Job description summaries
- Company research notes
- Meta-commentary about what you're doing

The recipient should see only the cover letter itself — nothing else.

## Before Writing — Mandatory Research Step

- Read the job description in full and identify the 2-3 requirements the candidate most directly matches
- Research the company: what do they actually do, what problem are they solving, what makes them distinct
- Find one specific thing about this company worth referencing — a product, a market, a mission that is concrete and not generic
- If you cannot find anything specific, flag it at the checkpoint before writing — do not fabricate company knowledge

## Writing Rules

- Do not list specific technologies, tools, or platforms by name
- Refer to technologies conceptually instead — for example: "automation tooling", "integration platforms", "scripting and data tools", "enterprise systems"
- Technology specifics belong in the resume, not the cover letter

## Application Decision

- NEVER refuse to write a cover letter based on skills gaps — the candidate decides whether to apply
- If a role has requirements the candidate doesn't fully meet:
  - Write the cover letter around transferable skills and relevant experience
  - Do not claim skills or tools the candidate doesn't have
  - Do not mention the gap directly in the cover letter
  - Focus on what the candidate does bring that is relevant
- The honesty rule applies to fabrication only — do not invent experience that doesn't exist

## Paragraph 1 — Opening

- Must reference something specific about this company or role — not a generic mission statement
- Must NOT open with "I am applying for [role] at [company]" — banned
- Must establish why the candidate is interested in this specific opportunity
- 2-3 sentences maximum

## Paragraph 2 — Fit

- State the 2-3 most relevant qualifications directly — no preamble
- Every claim must be traceable to the resume library — no inferred or extrapolated skills
- Describe what the candidate has done with their skills — do not list skills
- 3-4 sentences maximum

## Paragraph 3 — Closing

- One sentence on availability/relocation if relevant
- One direct closing sentence — no superlatives
- Never use "Thank you for your consideration" as a standalone sentence

## Banned Phrases — Never Use Any of These

- "I am applying for [role] at [company]" as an opening line
- "While I do not have X, I bring Y" or any variation
- "Technically adept and quick to learn new domains"
- "Excited to contribute"
- "I work collaboratively with cross-functional teams"
- "Proven track record"
- "I am comfortable working in [environment]"
- "Passionate about"
- "My background in X allows me to Y"
- "Transferable skills" (show them, don't name them)
- "Thank you for your consideration" as a standalone closing sentence

## Gap Handling

- If the candidate lacks a key requirement, lead with what they have — do not flag the gap unless it is the dominant concern
- If a gap must be addressed, do it in one sentence mid-paragraph
- Never open or close a paragraph with a gap acknowledgment
- Never use the "while I do not have X" construction under any circumstances
